bio Prof. Andrew White is an associate of professor at University of Rochester, a small private university in the northeastern US. His research group studies the self-assembly and structure of biomacromolecules with coarse-grained simulation and deep learning. He and his group are also leaders in the adaption of deep learning to chemistry and materials, with recent papers on structure-function deep learning models, advances in explainable neural networks, and AI-directed iterative experiments. They are also exploring the use of augmented reality in both research and teaching. White has won multiple awards, including Time magazine person of the year in 2006.
