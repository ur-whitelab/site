bio Andrew White is an associate of professor at University of Rochester, a small private university in the northeastern US. He received his PhD in chemical engineering from University of Washington and did a postdoc in chemistry at University of Chicago. White's research group studies the self-assembly and structure of biomacromolecules with coarse-grained simulation and deep learning. He and his group are leaders in the adaption of deep learning to chemistry and materials, with research on graph neural networks, explainablng deep learning models, and AI-directed iterative experiments. They are also exploring the use of augmented reality in both research and teaching. White has won multiple awards, including Time magazine person of the year in 2006.
